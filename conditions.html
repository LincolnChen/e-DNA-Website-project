Good and bad conditions for eDNA

1. Sunlight causes DNA to break down.

⇨

Good and bad conditions for eDNA

2. If eDNA is exposed to too much sunlight, it breaks down into smaller pieces which cannot be detected by the eDNA test.

⇦
⇨

Good and bad conditions for eDNA

3. Waterholes protected from sunlight by nearby cliffs or trees that provide shade may have better quality eDNA.

⇦
⇨

Good and bad conditions for eDNA

4. Dirty water can clog up filters when filtering water samples.

⇦
⇨

Good and bad conditions for eDNA

5. The eDNA in the sample then gets stuck and cannot be purified for testing.

⇦
⇨

Good and bad conditions for eDNA

6. Dirty water also contains more bacteria and enzymes.

⇦
⇨

Good and bad conditions for eDNA

7. Some bacteria and enzymes consume and break down DNA so it can no-longer be detected by the eDNA test.

⇦
⇨

Good and bad conditions for eDNA

8. If the amount of DNA in a sample is too low, it may not be found by the test.

⇦
⇨

Good and bad conditions for eDNA

9. A large waterhole may water down DNA making it harder to find.

⇦
⇨

Good and bad conditions for eDNA

10. Or, a small number of birds can lead to only a small amount of DNA being shed into the waterhole.

⇦
⇨

Good and bad conditions for eDNA

11. More DNA in a sample will lead to better results.

⇦
⇨

Good and bad conditions for eDNA

12. To increase chances of finding eDNA, at least three samples should be collected from each waterhole.

⇦
⇨

Good and bad conditions for eDNA

13. Samples must be collected under clean conditions.

⇦
⇨

Good and bad conditions for eDNA

14. When collecting samples gloves must be worn to ensure the samples are clean.

⇦
⇨

Good and bad conditions for eDNA

15. Dirty hands, gloves or bottles can contaminate the sample by adding DNA from other waterholes.

⇦
⇨

Good and bad conditions for eDNA

16. The best results are expected from waterholes that are shaded, clean, small and have lots of birds visiting!

⇦
⇨

Good and bad conditions for eDNA

17. Although some waterholes may have characteristics that are bad for eDNA, sometimes eDNA can still be found!

⇦
